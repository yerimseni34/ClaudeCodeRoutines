# Competitive Intelligence Report
## Wave Survival / Roguelite / Top-Down Shooter Genres
**Week 2026-23 | May 29 – June 5, 2026**

---

## THE BIG STORY THIS WEEK

**Steam Bullet Fest 2026 is the defining event of the moment.** Following Steam's official recognition of "Bullet Heaven" as a genre tag (May 18), Valve announced a dedicated Steam Bullet Fest running June 8–15. This is the first official Valve-sanctioned event for the genre, and it has triggered a launch cluster — at least 6 games in this report timed their Early Access debut or full launch to coincide with it. For a solo dev, this is the most important algorithmic tailwind in the genre's history.

## EMERGING TRENDS

1. **Extraction mechanics entering bullet heaven.** Hellforged is the pioneer. First-mover advantage real here; expect copycats in 6–12 months.

2. **Time-manipulation as a differentiator.** Three games this week use temporal mechanics differently: Grimloop (ghost army replay), DeadEye (time-stop combo chains), Decks of Dexterity (time-pause card turns). The genre is moving past pure auto-shooting into active player agency.

3. **Multi-weapon loadout depth over single-weapon evolution.** Arms of God (5 weapons simultaneously), FARLUME (15 weapons + 14 evolution forms), Sodaman (weapons + sodas + augments + deck). The "one weapon evolves into two" model feels dated — the market is rewarding systemic depth.

4. **Secondary mission layers inside runs.** Starforged Legacy adds missions mid-run. The flat "survive waves → pick upgrade → repeat" loop is being augmented with mini-objectives that add texture without needing full narrative infrastructure.

5. **Launch cluster effect around Bullet Fest.** Six+ games in this report timed launches to June 8–15. Opportunity (festival discovery) and risk (crowded window) in the same moment.

---

## THE GAP

**A manual-aim wave survival shooter with co-op at the $5–$10 price point.**

Most bullet heaven games are autoshooters. Arms of God offers aiming modes but is a solo experience. TerraTech Legion is vehicle-building. The underserved space: a top-down wave survival shooter where you aim manually, built around 2–4 player local/online co-op, priced at $7–$10, with a strong visual identity.

Deep Rock Galactic: Survivor is the genre's most successful recent comp — and it's solo only. The social/co-op version of that game doesn't exist at the indie price tier. That's the gap.

## ACTIONABLE INSIGHT FOR SOLO DEVS

**If your game is within 3–4 months of shippable: get a Steam Coming Soon page live and request Bullet Fest inclusion before June 8.**

Valve's Bullet Fest specifically features unreleased "Coming Soon" titles and games with demos. A game that's 60% done with a playable demo slice gets front-page genre event placement — the equivalent of tens of thousands in marketing spend. This window closes June 8.

**If you missed June 8:** launch during the post-Bullet Fest valley (June 16–30). The cluster of games launching June 8–15 will exhaust community attention and review bandwidth. A strong game launching June 18–25 faces dramatically less same-day competition while still capturing players who discovered the genre through the festival.

**Longer-term:** Study Hellforged's extraction hook. It's the first genuinely new emotional loop in the genre in two years. If you can find a comparable "one mechanical twist that changes the emotional arc," you have a marketing hook that writes itself.
