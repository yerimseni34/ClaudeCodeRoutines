# Competitor Watch — Week 2026-24 (June 8–14, 2026)
**Genre Focus:** Wave Survival Shooters · Roguelite Action · Top-Down Shooters · Isometric Action

> **Context:** Steam Bullet Fest ran June 8–15, 2026 — Valve's first themed sale event specifically for bullet hell, bullet heaven, twin-stick, and survivor-roguelite titles (~2,759 games discounted). This is the defining event of this week and dramatically amplifies signal-to-noise for the genre.

### 1. Wanted Shadows: Unchained
**Developer:** Conradical Games (small indie team)
**Release Status:** Released — June 8, 2026 (full launch, multi-platform)
**Platform:** PC; later Switch 2, PS5, PS4, Xbox Series X|S, Xbox One

**What happened this week:**
Full launch during Steam Bullet Fest. This is a ground-up remake of the 2023 original *Wanted Shadows*, rebuilt with compact high-intensity arenas, dual weapon combat (simultaneous melee + ranged), deepened progression, and an expanded hub world. The 2023 version is now 40% off, funneling curiosity traffic to the new release.

**Steam numbers:** Early community feedback from the demo noted fast-paced gameplay and pixel art positively; full launch review count not yet public.

**Momentum signal:** 🟢 Rising — remake framing is smart marketing (existing fanbase + new audience), Bullet Fest placement gives massive discovery surface, console ports signal commercial confidence.

**One thing to steal:** The "compact arena" design philosophy — instead of open maps, fights happen in tight, readable spaces. This concentrates action, reduces scope, and plays better in short sessions. Perfect for a solo dev constraint.

### 2. Sektori
**Developer:** Kimmo Lahtinen (solo dev)
**Release Status:** Released (featured/discounted in Steam Bullet Fest)
**Price:** 20% off during Bullet Fest

**What happened this week:**
Re-spotlighted by Kotaku's "10 Games to Play in Steam Bullet Fest" list. A twin-stick wave survival shooter with music-synchronized combat and hand-crafted attack wave layering.

**Steam numbers:** 769 reviews, **96% positive**. Metro awarded it **9/10** and called it "one of the greatest twin-stick shooters ever made."

**Momentum signal:** 🟢 Sustained high — 96% with 769 reviews is rare territory. A solo dev game holding this rating under Bullet Fest spotlight is meaningful. Not a new release, but freshly re-amplified.

**One thing to steal:** Hand-crafted wave layering + randomized combination. Rather than fully procedural enemies, Lahtinen designs discrete wave "modules" that combine with upgrade systems to create emergent variety. The seams stay invisible because each module was authored. This is the technique that keeps Sektori feeling intentional rather than random.

### 3. Relic Abyss
**Developer:** Reverse Loop (3-person team, Philippines)
**Release Status:** Early Access since April 13, 2026; free demo live during Bullet Fest
**Price:** $7.19 (20% off, normally $8.99) — price increase planned at 1.0 launch

**What happened this week:**
Free demo dropped this week timed to Steam Bullet Fest. The demo is also available on itch.io. Strong social amplification within Filipino game dev communities. New Instagram reel promoting the demo release.

**Steam numbers:** 80 reviews overall (81% positive / Very Positive); Recent reviews 100% positive (17 reviews). Published by Mameshiba Games.

**Momentum signal:** 🟢 Rising — review velocity accelerating (recent 100% vs overall 81% suggests positive word of mouth growing). Filipino dev community providing organic PR. Sub-$10 price is a strong impulse-buy signal during a sale event.

**One thing to steal:** JRPG-style skill breadth in a bullet heaven wrapper. 134 unique skills across 20 playable characters gives players massive build diversity without the game needing to be long. The Lovecraftian + medieval sci-fi setting is unusual enough to stand out on a crowded tag page. Unusual art direction beats more-polished-generic every time in discovery.

### 4. Hell Bug
**Developer:** Artisan Knights Studios
**Release Status:** Released — June 8, 2026 (15% launch discount, demo available)

**What happened this week:**
Launch window during Bullet Fest. Arcade roguelite structure: handcrafted stages selected randomly, wide arsenal of weapons, and a boss-unlock mechanic where defeated bosses become playable characters with their own inherited abilities. Demo lets players reach The Rhino boss.

**Steam numbers:** Full launch review count not yet substantial; demo available on Steam separately.

**Momentum signal:** 🟡 Neutral/Early — too new to read. The boss-unlock loop is the headline hook, which is a strong differentiator if the demo communicates it clearly. Bullet Fest is the right event to be launching in.

**One thing to steal:** Boss-as-playable-character unlocks. Defeating the boss *is* the reward, but then *becoming* the boss for the next run is the retention hook. This is a low-content-cost, high-feel-reward system — each boss just needs a character select screen entry and reskinned ability set. The psychological payoff per hour of dev work is excellent.

## Weekly Insight

### Trend Emerging This Week
**Steam is formalizing the "bullet" subgenre as a first-class event category.** Bullet Fest 2026 is the proof-of-concept that Valve is willing to create dedicated sale infrastructure for bullet hell / bullet heaven / survivors-like titles — following the addition of the "Bullet Heaven" Steam tag in May 2026. This legitimizes the subgenre in the same way the "Deckbuilders Fest" did for card games. Expect dedicated algorithmic surface area (curated lists, event hubs, search boosts) to persist beyond the sale window.

The **deckbuilder + survivors hybrid** is the dominant genre experiment right now (Decks of Dexterity, Forge the Fates, Vampire Survivors spinoff in development). This is the moment just before that subgenre becomes crowded.

### Gap Visible This Week
The Bullet Fest coverage reveals a clear underserved niche: **structured-session wave survival with a strong setting** (not endless, not purely procedural). Ignitement's 20-minute arena format, Wanderburg's 6-hour demo retention, and Sektori's hand-authored wave modules all point to the same gap: *players want crafted intensity arcs, not infinite treadmills*. Most new entrants are still defaulting to "survive as long as possible" — the games breaking out are the ones with authored escalation and a clear end state.

Additionally: **zero games this week shipped a wave survival roguelite set in a non-fantasy, non-sci-fi setting** (urban, historical, mythological, food, nature). The art direction on every entry this week defaults to fantasy or sci-fi. A distinctive visual identity in an unusual setting is a free differentiator.

### One Actionable Insight for a Solo Dev
**Build your demo first, ship it to GX Games immediately, use Bullet Fest (or the next equivalent event) as your launch window.** Decks of Dexterity's 2.5M demo plays before launch shows that browser-accessible demos on GX Games are a pre-warming channel that Steam doesn't offer. The cost of porting a demo to web is lower than it sounds (Godot HTML5 export, Unity WebGL). By the time you launch on Steam, you have an audience that already knows the game loop. The demo on Steam gets 30-60 minute sessions; the GX Games demo with the right game gets millions.
